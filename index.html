Ichu River Watershed

The upper Ichu river watershed (UIRW), located in Southwestern Huancavelica, Peru, is part of the Mantaro Basin. It encompasses an area of 1383.8 square kilometers draining into the Mantaro River. The UIRW is characterized by presenting high peaks and mountains in the Southern part and narrow valleys in the northern. The highest elevation corresponds to 17,273.6 feet (5,265 masl), while the lowest one corresponds to 8,202.1 feet (2500 masl) at the confluence with the Mantaro River. Characterized by having perennial flow, UIRW is considered one of the main tributaries of the middle Mantaro River basin, which is the prominent River in Peru. The mayor area of UIRW is located across Huancavelica and Castrovirreyna provinces (97.4%), and 1.3% across Other provinces.

The UIRW climate corresponds to a mountain climate with the lack of rains in summer and mild rains in winter. Between November and March, higher precipitations take place. Snow scarcity causes large flows in rivers during the winter months. The little presence of snow causes the large flows in the rivers to occur during the winter months. UIRW soils are mainly composed of fine soils such as silt and clay with abundant natural phosphate. Due to the predominance of fine soils, upstream areas are vulnerable to erosion and landslides phenomena. In agricultural activity areas, this has been further aggravated. Measurements of water quality at upstream areas showed better results; however, as one reaches medium and lower areas (agricultural areas), water quality degrades rapidly, showing increments in water temperature and higher phosphorus concentrations than natural ones. Some streams such as the Cachimayo and Astobamba show lower Dissolved Oxygen (DO) levels than natural conditions, limiting aquatic life. Although improvement concerning DO has been observed in some streams, temperatures remain with the same poor quality. Regarding land use, there are three dominated areas: the northern half area is dominated by forestry involving around 55% of the UIRW, the center part is dominated by agriculture that encompasses around 40%, and the southern part is dominated by a growing urban area by around 5%.

The upstream part of the UIRW is dominated by long-lived trees species such as evergreen forest and shrubland, while the downstream part is dominated by seasonal crops such as Slender Wheatgrass, and at the most downstream extent, is dominated by urban areas.

Potential Storage

This image shows the various sites (depicted by polygons) on agricultural land where runoff will tend to accumulate because of depression in the topography, and low infiltration properties of the soil. Though the landscape also has many smaller sites where runoff accumulation can occur,this image only shows sites with areas of polygons greater or equal to 1000 square meters (i.e. 0.25 acres). These sites provide excellent opportunities for creating water storage practices such as wetlands, ponds, etc. that slow, store, and treat runoff. This helps in not only preventing erosion and fertilizer losses, but also helps improve availability of water in the landscape for crop use during dry seasons.
